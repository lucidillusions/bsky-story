>“It soon brought back the paralysis. It wasn’t just the heaviness during sleep; it was a granite weight pressing on his chest, pinning him to the mattress. He would wake into a world that was almost exactly like his own, yet wrong in its stillness. In the past, he saw only darkness, but now, he saw movement.”

<hr />

When I sent out my submission, I didn't realise Novels & Nightmares Narrative were looking to select one story per zine, and I'm so happy and excited for my story to be the winning selection.

Check out the First Issue of N&N Narrative and my story [A Gnawing Silence of my Sleep Demon](https://www.patreon.com/NandNNarrative/posts/june-newsletter-160856586) on their Patreon. The issue also highlights an indie horror book called "[Blood On The Boardwalk](https://amzn.to/4ej0UCW)".

<hr />

The story has gone through multiple iterations, the original story was stemmed from a simple idea, what visceral memory of a child could give shape to his sleep demon, followed by what if the sleep demon actually becomes a real demon. The second idea that I smashed into this OG idea—what if the MC is a horror story writer, and the sleep demon is not letting him finish the story?

This was originally written as a flash fiction (with a different sleep demon) but I reworked it to write a longer piece to send out to A Midnight Kind of Place for their Anthology call! It received an R along with the other 3 stories I wrote for various Antho calls for AMKOP.

Honestly I feel N&N Narratives is a great home for the story! and yes, you should read it now, and also check out how cool a project Novels and Nightmares really is!
